The Quran, the holy book of Islam, is considered the literal word of God (Allah) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) over a period of approximately 23 years, beginning in 610 CE. It is written in classical Arabic and is divided into 114 chapters known as surahs, which vary in length.   • Worship: The Quran is integral to Muslim worship, especially in the five daily prayers (Salah) where verses are recited.
  • Guidance: It provides guidance for personal conduct, family life, business dealings, and social justice.
  • Education: Quranic education starts early in a Muslim's life, with emphasis on memorization, understanding, and implementation of its teachings.

The Quran was revealed during a time of significant social, political, and religious change in the Arabian Peninsula. It addressed the existing societal issues and provided a framework for a just and moral society. The Quran's revelation marked the beginning of a new era in Arabian history, influencing not only religious practices but also law, culture, and governance.

In summary, the Quran is the central religious text of Islam, revered as the word of God and a comprehensive guide for all aspects of life. Its study, recitation, and application are fundamental to the faith and practice of Muslims worldwide.
